# Summary Report: “FCAJ Community Day”

### Event Objectives

* Learn from the experiences of senior members in the FCAJ community.
* Broaden perspectives on learning, AI, cloud computing, professional mindset, and software development methodologies.
* Network with the technical community.
* Define a clear learning and personal development path during the internship.

* **Mr. Long Huynh** - Admin of FCAJ - *Why does your brain choose social media over studying?*

### Key Highlights

#### Mr. Long Huynh - Why does your brain choose social media over studying?

This session explored the reasons why our brains are easily drawn to social media and games rather than studying. The primary reason is that these activities offer instant rewards, stimulate curiosity, and require less mental energy. In contrast, studying often yields delayed results and requires sustained focus, which easily leads to procrastination.

The speaker explained the mechanics of dopamine and how social media creates a continuous anticipation of rewards. To counter this, learners can make studying more engaging by:
* Creating small rewards after achieving milestones.
* Maintaining study streaks to build motivation.
* Breaking down learning goals and starting with micro-tasks, such as studying for just 10 minutes or reading a single page.

The key takeaway is that effective learners don't just rely on sheer willpower; instead, they build an environment and habits that make them want to study every day.

#### Mr. Thinh Nguyen - Automated Prompt Engineering: Enhancing LLM Output Quality

This was a highly detailed presentation on Prompt Engineering—the art of communicating with AI to receive higher-quality outputs. A generic prompt will yield a generic response; vague instructions will lead to inconsistent results. Poorly constructed prompts can also waste tokens, thereby reducing productivity and increasing costs.

A well-crafted prompt should include key components: **Role**, **Instruction/Task**, **Context**, **Input Data**, **Output Format**, **Examples**, and **Constraints/Guidelines**.

The speaker shared crucial best practices, including:
* Using clear, specific, and directive language.
* Utilizing delimiters to separate different sections within a prompt.
* Describing what the AI *should* do rather than what it shouldn't.
* Allowing the AI to answer "I don't know" when information is lacking.
* Breaking down excessively long inputs into smaller chunks.

Regarding Token Economics, LLMs process content by tokens, and costs depend on the input/output token volume. The number of tokens consumed can also vary depending on the language used. To optimize further, advanced prompting techniques can be applied, such as Chain-of-Thought, Self-Consistency, Tree-of-Thoughts, Retrieval-Augmented Generation, and Role Prompting.

During the session, the speaker introduced the **Proptimizer** project—a browser extension designed to optimize prompts and assist with AI chats directly on the web. The solution architecture of Proptimizer is fully built on AWS services, including CloudFront, S3, Cognito, API Gateway, Lambda, Bedrock, DynamoDB, and CloudWatch. This practical knowledge is highly valuable, helping students and interns use AI more effectively when learning AWS, writing documentation, debugging, and handling daily technical tasks.

#### Mr. Khang Nguyen - AI-Ready Freshers: Skills and Mindset in the AI Era

This presentation emphasized that freshers in the AI era should not blindly chase after new tools, but rather focus on building a solid foundation. AI acts as a capability amplifier: if you have a strong foundation, AI will help you work better and faster; however, if you lack an understanding of the core principles, AI can produce poor and uncontrollable results.

When tackling assignments, projects, or interviews, the final result is not the only thing that matters. The thought process and the reasoning behind choosing a specific solution are far more critical.

The speaker highlighted the importance of asking "why", questioning one's own understanding, and accepting mistakes in order to learn from them. When entering the actual workforce, attributes such as integrity, long-term thinking, networking, teamwork skills, and the ability to ask the right questions are the most vital keys to success.

#### Ms. Thao - BMAD Method: Bring your entire Agile & AI team into your IDE

This topic centered around the BMAD Method and how to integrate a structured software development process into coding with AI. "Vibe coding" (coding spontaneously with AI) can help create products quickly, but if the software development lifecycle is ignored, it can easily lead to messy, unmaintainable, and uncontrollable code.

If we continuously prompt AI without proper documentation, architecture, and planning, the AI will gradually lose context and generate fragmented code. To address this, the BMAD method helps organize the workflow structurally.

The process includes: brainstorming, creating a PRD (Product Requirements Document), drafting an architecture document, dividing epics/stories, and finally utilizing development and QA/review agents. Breaking down tasks and assigning specific roles to agents helps the AI understand the context clearly, thereby reducing hallucinations and allowing humans to maintain better control over the entire software development process. The main lesson learned is that when using AI to code, clear documentation and processes are just as important as the prompts themselves.

### Key Takeaways

* **Sustainable learning habits**: Understanding brain mechanics to build an effective learning environment and habits, maintaining long-term motivation rather than relying on temporary effort.
* **Prompt engineering and effective AI usage**: Mastering the components of a standard prompt, best practices, and advanced techniques to optimize the output quality of language models.
* **Foundation, thought process, and questioning skills**: Recognizing the critical importance of foundational knowledge, problem-solving mindset, and continuous learning attitude in the AI era.
* **BMAD Method and systematic software development**: Grasping how to integrate AI into the software development lifecycle structurally, using documentation and task division to control code quality.
* **The value of the FCAJ community**: Realizing the immense benefits of sharing knowledge, practical experiences, and expanding one's network with industry professionals.

### Applying to the Internship

* Apply consistent learning habits to complete Worklogs, update Event Participated sections, and finish the remaining parts of the internship report.
* Utilize clearer, more structured prompt engineering techniques when studying AWS services, writing technical documentation, analyzing errors (debugging), or researching new services.
* When working on projects or workshops, focus not only on the final outcome but also on refining the thought process: clearly understanding the rationale behind architectural choices, related AWS services, and workflows.
* Apply task breakdown methods, prepare thorough documentation, and maintain strict context control when using AI to assist in project development.

### Event Experience

This was one of the first events I attended during my AWS FCAJ Cloud Intern program. The event provided a comprehensive experience by seamlessly combining technical aspects and mindset: from learning methodologies, AI applications, cloud knowledge, and prompt engineering, to career mindset orientation and software development methodology.

I was particularly impressed by the Prompt Engineering session and the Proptimizer practical project, as this content directly addressed the challenges I have been facing when using AI for learning and daily technical tasks.

Through these sharing sessions, I realized that learning cloud computing or any technology is not merely about learning how to use a tool. To succeed in the long run, I need to build a rock-solid foundation, hone my questioning skills, develop sustainable learning capabilities, and adopt a systematic, disciplined approach to work.
